About

Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and the former chairman of the Tata Group, one of Indiaâ€™s biggest conglomerates. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential business leaders of modern India, known for his strategic vision, corporate leadership, and philanthropic efforts.

Early Life and Education

Ratan Tata was born into the prominent Tata family, which is known for its contributions to the Indian economy and society. He studied at the prestigious Campion School, Mumbai, and later pursued Architecture at Cornell University, USA. He also holds an advanced management degree from the Harvard Business School.

Leadership at Tata Group

Ratan Tata became the chairman of Tata Group in 1990, succeeding his uncle, J.R.D. Tata. Under his leadership, Tata Group expanded its global presence with landmark acquisitions such as Tetley, Corus, and Jaguar Land Rover, transforming it into a global business powerhouse. His ability to balance innovation with ethical business practices made Tata Group a respected name worldwide.
